Policy Recommendations

Based on our ethical analysis and case studies, we propose the following policy interventions to mitigate harm while preserving beneficial uses of FRT:

Immediate Actions

  • Moratoriums on public FRT: Following models like San Francisco's 2019 ban until proper safeguards exist
  • Bias auditing requirements: Mandate third-party testing for demographic disparities
  • Transparency laws: Require disclosure of training data sources and accuracy metrics

Medium-Term Reforms

  • Opt-in consent models: Prohibit non-consensual commercial use
  • Accuracy standards: Set minimum performance thresholds across demographics
  • Right to explanation: Require human review of algorithmic decisions affecting rights

Long-Term Solutions

  • Federal privacy legislation: Comprehensive data protection laws covering biometrics
  • Public oversight: Citizen review boards for surveillance technologies
  • Algorithmic accountability: Liability frameworks for harmful deployments

Technological Improvements

Beyond policy, technical approaches can reduce harm:

Debiasing Algorithms

Developers must prioritize diverse training datasets and techniques like adversarial debiasing to minimize demographic disparities.

On-Device Processing

Local facial analysis (without centralized databases) could reduce mass surveillance risks while enabling useful applications.

Explainable AI

Making matching decisions interpretable helps users understand and contest identifications.
